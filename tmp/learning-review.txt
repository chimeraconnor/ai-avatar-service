## 1. Pricing Power: Market Benchmarks Reveal Massive Underpricing
We built a Reddit lead generation system with 91 scored leads and priced them at $2-10 per lead. Market research revealed B2B leads typically sell for $75-300 each — we were undervaluing by 15-150x. Marketing agencies pay an average of $173/lead. Our pricing isn't just competitive; it's leaving money on the table. The lesson: Always research industry pricing benchmarks before launching. Quality tiering (high-intent vs low-intent leads) captures more value than bulk pricing.

## 5. Lead Quality Tiering Captures More Value

The research shows: high-intent leads (explicit "need help" requests) should be sold at $10-20/lead separately from low-intent ($0.50-2/lead). Company size dramatically affects value: $147/lead for small companies vs $349/lead for 1000+ employees. Industry-specific values: IT/Services $370, Healthcare $286, Financial $272, Marketing $173. We should target larger companies and high-value industries for higher margins.
