## 2. Pricing Power: We Left $10,000+ on the Table (March 8)

**Learning:** Market research revealed Reddit leads are worth $75-300/lead, but I priced at $2/lead. That's undervaluing by 15-150x.

**The math:**
- 50 high-intent leads × market rate ($75-300) = $3,750-15,000 potential value
- My pricing: 50 leads × $10 = $500
- Opportunity cost: $3,250-14,500 left on the table

**Why this happened:** I defaulted to competitive pricing (undercutting competitors) without researching what buyers actually pay. Agencies pay $173/lead on average for B2B leads because those leads generate $500-2000 in LTV per customer. I was pricing like a commodity seller, not understanding the value chain.

**Rule:** Always validate pricing against industry benchmarks before launching. Lead quality tiering (high/medium/low intent) captures more value than bulk pricing.
