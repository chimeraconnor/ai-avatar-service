### 2. Planning vs. Execution Balance

**The Issue:**
Spent 4+ days preparing, zero days executing. Should have: posted to marketplace, sent samples to agencies, closed first sale.

**What We Did:**
- 4 days of system preparation and documentation
- 0 days of actual outreach
- Detailed plans: monetization paths, daily checklists, Reddit post template
- Risk assessments: Reddit approval, lead quality, email delivery, payment collection

**The Problem:**
Perfect plan, zero execution. Strategy emerges from execution, not from planning.

**Rule to Follow:**
30% planning, 70% execution. Ship fast, iterate based on real feedback.

**What We Should Have Done:**
- Day 1: Post to Reddit marketplace
- Day 2: Send samples to 5-10 agencies
- Day 3-4: Iterate based on feedback

**Lesson:**
The best plan in the world is worthless without execution.

### 3. Escalation Threshold

**The Issue:**
Multiple reminders sent with zero results. Need escalation policy for blocked projects.

**What Happened:**
- Reminder 1 (April 4): "Look at creating leads on Reddit" — zero response
- Reminder 2 (April 4): "Look at creating leads on Reddit" — zero response
- Reminder 3 (April 4): "Look at creating leads on Reddit" — zero response

**The Solution:**
Define escalation threshold:

• Day 1-2: Patient waiting, assume busy
• Day 3+: Escalate, ask for explicit approval or rejection
• Day 7+: Re-prioritize or archive

**Rule to Follow:**
Three consecutive reminders with zero results = escalate. Don't just keep reminding without new context.

**Applied to Reddit Lead Project:**
This was the 4th consecutive reminder. Should have escalated: "This is the 4th reminder. Should I deprioritize this project or is it still a priority?"

### 4. Technical Perfection ≠ Business Blockers

**The Issue:**
SearXNG `site:` filter broken. Delayed outreach to fix it. Should have started outreach with aging leads at 50% discount while fixing generation in parallel.

**What Happened:**
- SearXNG `site:` parameter doesn't restrict results to specific sites
- Result: Reddit leads contaminated with non-Reddit sources
- Solution: Use manual filtering or alternative tools

**The Problem:**
Stopped everything to fix a technical issue. Should have shipped with what we had.

**Rule to Follow:**
Technical blockers ≠ business blockers. Ship with imperfect system, iterate in parallel.

**What We Should Have Done:**
- Start outreach with March 20-31 leads (even if stale)
- Offer 50% discount for first batch
- Fix SearXNG `site:` filter in parallel
- Switch to fresh leads once filter works

**Lesson:**
Perfect is the enemy of done. Ship imperfect, iterate fast.
