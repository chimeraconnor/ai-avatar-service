## Research Methodology

### Step 1: Seed Keyword Identification

**What are seed keywords?**
- Broad terms related to your business, product, or topic
- 1-3 words typically
- High search volume, high competition
- Starting point for research

**How to find seed keywords**:
1. **Brainstorming**: List 10-20 core terms
2. **Website audit**: What does your site already rank for?
3. **Competitor analysis**: What are competitors targeting?
4. **Customer language**: How do customers describe your products?
5. **Google Search**: Type seed term and see suggestions

**Examples**:
- SEO agency: "SEO services", "SEO company", "search engine optimization"
- E-commerce (shoes): "running shoes", "basketball shoes", "sneakers"
- SaaS (keyword tool): "keyword research", "SEO tools", "keyword finder"

### Step 2: Keyword Expansion

**Expansion Methods**:

**Method A: Autocomplete Mining**
```
1. Type seed keyword into Google
2. Record all autocomplete suggestions
3. Add letters a-z to seed and repeat
4. Collect 50-100 variations
```

**Method B: People Also Ask (PAA) Chain Mining**
```
1. Search seed keyword
2. Click first PAA question to expand
3. Record new PAA questions
4. Repeat for each new question (3-4 levels deep)
5. Organize by topic and intent
```

**Method C: Related Searches**
```
1. Scroll to bottom of Google SERP
2. Note "searches related to..." terms
3. Use these as new seed keywords
4. Repeat process
```

**Method D: Keyword Tool Database**
- Use SEMrush/Ahrefs Keyword Magic Tool
- Start with seed keyword
- Filter by volume, difficulty, word count
- Save 50-100 relevant keywords

### Step 3: Intent Classification

**Classify each keyword by search intent**:

**Informational**:
- Keywords: "how to", "guide", "tutorial", "what is"
- User goal: Learn, research
- Content: Guides, tutorials, listicles
- Example: "how to do keyword research"

**Transactional**:
- Keywords: "buy", "price", "cheap", "best for"
- User goal: Purchase, sign up
- Content: Product pages, comparisons, reviews
- Example: "best keyword research tool under $50"

**Navigational**:
- Keywords: Brand names, specific product names
- User goal: Find specific site/page
- Content: Optimize brand terms, clear navigation
- Example: "SEMrush keyword magic tool"

**Commercial Investigation**:
- Keywords: "[X] vs [Y]", "alternatives to", "top 10"
- User goal: Compare options before purchase
- Content: Comparison articles, "best of" lists
- Example: "Ahrefs vs SEMrush keyword research"

### Step 4: Difficulty & Volume Analysis

**Metrics to collect**:

**Search Volume (SV)**:
- Monthly average searches
- Filter based on your site size:
  - New site: 100-1,000 SV
  - Established site: 1,000-10,000 SV
  - Authority site: 10,000+ SV

**Keyword Difficulty (KD)**:
- Scale: 0-100 (harder to rank)
- Filter based on your authority:
  - Low authority (DA < 20): KD 0-20
  - Medium authority (DA 20-40): KD 21-40
  - High authority (DA > 40): KD 41-60+

**CPC (Cost Per Click)**:
- Proxy for commercial intent
- Higher CPC = higher purchase intent
- Use to prioritize transactional keywords

**Competition**:
- Analyze top 10 results
- Look at:
  - Domain authority (Moz DR, Ahrefs DR)
  - Content quality and depth
  - Content freshness
  - Brand presence

### Step 5: Keyword Prioritization

**Opportunity Score Formula**:
```
Opportunity Score = (Search Volume × Click Potential × Business Relevance) ÷ (Keyword Difficulty × Content Production Difficulty)
```

**Scoring Guide**:

**Business Relevance (1-10)**:
- 10: Directly addresses your product/service
- 7-9: Related to your industry
- 4-6: Loosely related
- 1-3: Tangential

**Content Production Difficulty (1-10)**:
- 1: Existing content, minor edit
- 3: Quick article (500-1,000 words)
- 5: Standard article (1,000-2,000 words)
- 7: Comprehensive guide (2,000-3,000 words)
- 10: Complex research + writing (3,000+ words)

**Prioritization**:
1. Filter out irrelevant keywords (relevance < 4)
2. Calculate opportunity score
3. Sort by score (highest first)
4. Focus on top 20%
5. Balance short-term wins (high score, low difficulty) with long-term goals (high volume)

## Advanced Techniques

### SERP Volatility Exploitation

**Identify volatile keywords**:
- Track rankings over 30 days
- Look for:
  - Position changes of 3+ spots in 7 days
  - New SERP features appearing/disappearing
  - New domains entering top 10

**Strategy**:
- Create fresher content than competitors
- Address recent developments
- Apply enhanced E-E-A-T signals
- Promote aggressively during volatility

**Opportunity**: Volatile keywords are 2.8x easier to rank for during fluctuations

### Topic Clusters & Semantic SEO

**Topic Cluster Method**:
1. Identify primary topic (short-tail, high volume)
2. Find related secondary topics
3. Map semantic relationships
4. Create pillar page (3,000+ words)
5. Create cluster articles (1,500+ words each)
6. Link pillar → clusters, clusters → pillar

**Entity Optimization**:
- Identify key entities in your niche
- Mention related entities together
- Establish semantic connections
- Reduce dependency on exact-match keywords by 67%

### Local SEO Keyword Strategy

**"Near Me" Variations**:
- "keyword research agency near me"
- "SEO services near me"
- Highest local intent, often voice-activated

**Location-Specific Keywords**:
- "keyword research agency in [City]"
- "SEO services in [City, State]"
- Target specific geographic areas

**Service-Specific Keywords**:
- "keyword research for [industry]"
- "SEO services for [business type]"
- Target ideal clients

## Workflow Checklist

### Research Checklist

- [ ] Identify 10-20 seed keywords
- [ ] Expand to 50-100 keywords using multiple methods
- [ ] Classify each by search intent
- [ ] Collect volume, difficulty, CPC metrics
- [ ] Analyze top 10 competitors for each keyword
- [ ] Calculate opportunity score for each keyword
- [ ] Prioritize top 20% by opportunity score
- [ ] Identify PAA questions for content
- [ ] Map topic clusters for authority building
- [ ] Create keyword tracking spreadsheet

### Validation Checklist

- [ ] Are keywords relevant to business goals?
- [ ] Is search intent understood?
- [ ] Is volume sufficient for your site size?
- [ ] Is difficulty within your authority range?
- [ ] Are there clear SERP opportunities?
- [ ] Is competition beatable with current resources?
- [ ] Have you considered local/long-tail variations?
